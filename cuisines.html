    Alfredo Pasta

  • Alfredo Pasta might have remained a local Roman specialty if it were not for Hollywood actors Douglas Fairbanks and Mary Pickford, who visited Rome on their honeymoon in the 1920s. They dined at Alfredos restaurant, fell in love with the dish, and reportedly brought the recipe back to the United States.

    Mandi

  • Mandi is a classic dish that hails from Yemen and has gained widespread popularity across the Arabian Peninsula and beyond, particularly in countries such as Saudi Arabia, Oman, and the United Arab Emirates. The dish is renowned for its unique cooking method, where meat, typically lamb or chicken, is slow-cooked with rice, allowing the flavors to blend deeply and create a rich, savory taste.

    Pizza

  • The concept of pizza begins with the use of flatbreads in ancient civilizations. Both the Egyptians, Greeks, and Romans ate bread topped with oils, herbs, and other ingredients. These early flatbreads resemble what we now consider pizza, though they were much simpler.

    Shawerma plater

  • Shawarma's history is a testament to the cultural fusion that defines Middle Eastern cuisine. From its Ottoman origins to becoming a global favorite, Arab shawarma has captured the hearts and palates of people worldwide, offering a perfect blend of tradition and modern adaptability.

    fish & chips

  • The introduction of fried fish to Britain is often credited to Jewish immigrants from Spain and Portugal who settled in England in the 17th century. These Jews, known as Sephardic Jews, prepared fish by frying it in oil, a practice known as "pescado frito". This cooking method was adapted in Britain and became popular, especially in London and coastal towns.
